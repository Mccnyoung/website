Spark

Design a goal tracking app based on user data and develop a design system

overview

Every year, people set New Year’s resolutions, but 92% of them end in failure. To uncover the reasons behind this and offer solutions that help people stay on track with their goals, we started project Spark.

I was involved in the end-to-end process, from service planning to product design, with a primary focus on branding design and optimizing the goal-setting process. Our team is currently working with developers to launch an MVP by the end of the year.

Summary

Grow a specific and small goal gradually

Most goal-tracking apps focus on extrinsic motivation like rewards rather than on setting goals. This leads users to set overly ambitious or vague goals. We designed a service that focuses on gradually growing a specific goal from the start, ultimately increasing the goal achievement rate.

Challenge

Find a way to maintain
people’s behavior

Most people begin their goals with strong willpower, yet maintaining that momentum can be challenging, often resulting in abandonment. This project aimed to help people enjoyably pursue their goals by providing engaging tools that keep them motivated and on track.

Discovery

Customer Insights

Set multiple, difficult goals at the start

External motivation leads users to set multiple and challenging
goals, but their enthusiasm fades and results in failure.

Randomly add recommended goals

Recommended goals were added without strong intent,
leading to quick abandonment.

Tracking goals feels like a chore

Users want a quick way to log their progress
but found the process cumbersome.

Ignore push notifications

Both services use push notifications to remind users.
However, people often ignore them, leading to fatigue.

Design System

Ensure consistency

We built and documented a design system to reduce redundant asset creation and improve development productivity. This ensures a consistent ux across all interfaces and enables clear communication with developers.

Solution

Introducing Spark

Guide to set small, measurable goals

To prevent setting overly ambitious goals early on and facing failure, Spark helps users establish small, measurable goals during the initial setup.

Achieve goals gradually through levels

To avoid the pitfalls of setting unrealistic goals, Spark divides progress into four levels, enabling users to gradually increase their goal difficulty over time.

Make goal tracking fun with
engaging motion-based interaction

To encourage users to voluntarily enter the app without push notifications, Spark incorporates interactive features like shaking the device to log goals.
